## Overview
This project documents an internal security audit conducted for a fictional company, Botium Toys. The purpose of this audit was to assess the organization's current security posture, identify risks, and evaluate existing security controls and compliance requirements.

This audit follows the National Institute of Standards and Technology Cybersecurity Framework (NIST CSF).

## Scenario Summary
Botium Toys is a small U.S.-based company with a growing online presence. As the business expanded internationally, concerns arose regarding the protection of customer data, payment information, and compliance with regulations such as GDPR and PCI DSS.

An internal security audit was initiated to:
- Identify security risks
- Evaluate existing controls
- Assess compliance requirements
- Recommend improvements

## Audit Scope
- Digital and physical assets
- User access and permissions
- Security controls and procedures
- Regulatory compliance

## Frameworks & Standards Used
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
- CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability)
- GDPR
- PCI DSS

## Key Outcomes
- Identified high-risk security gaps
- Evaluated administrative, technical, and physical controls
- Assessed compliance readiness
- Developed risk mitigation recommendations
